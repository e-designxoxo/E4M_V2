Who we are · Our story

Built on one belief.
Education changes everything.

Founded by Malagasy educators and international partners, Education for Madagascar has grown from a single classroom in Antananarivo into a four-country NGO running 13 programmes across education, health and the environment.

Our history

From one school to four countries

2016
United Kingdom

Charity incorporated in England & Wales

Education for Madagascar is registered as a Charity Incorporated Organisation (CIO) in England and Wales — Charity No. 1167115 — on 16th May 2016. The first formal legal structure, built around the conviction that quality education is the most durable route out of poverty.

2017
Madagascar

Malagasy NGO registered in Antananarivo

The organisation is formally registered as a Malagasy NGO on 24th May 2017, renewed in July 2023. NIF 4002796057. The AKKA Montessori school opens its first classroom — the seed of everything that follows.

2018
France

Association France & a patron joins

A French association is registered (SIREN 85242793900016), broadening the organisation's European reach. Grégoire Mulliez becomes Patron of Education for Madagascar — on behalf of a long-standing friendship, and to the wellbeing of humanity.

2022
Switzerland

Fourth entity: Association Suisse

An association is registered in Geneva, extending the network to Switzerland. With four legal entities across three continents, the organisation can raise and deploy resources across a wider base of supporters and institutions.

2025
Global

UNESCO World Heritage partnership announced

A new partnership with UNESCO is announced — expanding the organisation's reach into World Heritage education. The 13th active programme, and a signal of the organisation's growing international credibility.

Our mission

To relieve poverty in Madagascar through education

"Education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the world."— Nelson Mandela

In Madagascar, poverty and education are deeply intertwined. Children leave school because of hunger. Girls drop out because of gender inequality. Families can't afford healthcare that would keep children learning. Our response is a connected system: education at the centre, with health, food, water and environmental programmes removing the obstacles that stop learning from taking hold.

👤
Our patron since 2018

Grégoire Mulliez

Grégoire Mulliez has been the patron of Education for Madagascar since 2018 — on behalf of a long-standing friendship, and to the wellbeing of humanity. His support has been a steady and generous anchor for the organisation's growth.

What guides us

Three principles, every decision

01

Quality

We believe in the Montessori principle: a child who loves to learn will keep learning. Every programme is designed around genuine educational quality, not just attendance numbers.

02

Ethics

We work with Malagasy communities, not for them. Local leadership, transparent governance and honest reporting are non-negotiable — to our beneficiaries, our partners and our donors.

03

Sharing

Knowledge, resources and skills flow in both directions. Volunteers learn from Madagascar; Madagascar gains from the world. That exchange is the foundation of lasting change.
