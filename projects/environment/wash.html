Clean Water & Sanitation

WASH

Water is not a luxury. In Madagascar, it is a matter of survival — and of justice.

Environment UN SDG 6 Dry toilets 12m³ saved / person / year Ambohibe · Morondava
6 Clean Water
and Sanitation

Committed to UN Sustainable Development Goal 6

Clean water and sanitation is one of the 17 global goals set by the United Nations. Madagascar is one of its most urgent frontiers — and WASH is Education for Madagascar's direct response.

The reality on the ground

Water scarcity is a serious issue in the world.
In Madagascar, it is critical.

On the hill of Ambohibe, drinking water is rare. A single toilet flush wastes more water than a family has access to in a day. The consequences reach far beyond thirst — unmanaged wastewater, soil degradation, disease, and the loss of the very humus that could feed the next generation.

Our solution

Two models of dry toilet — one purpose

Model 01 — Basic

The Wood Chip Toilet

How it works

Wood chips or sawdust are added after each use. They absorb moisture and eliminate odours naturally — no chemicals, no water, no smell.

✓ No water used. Output goes directly to the compost bin, which feeds the permaculture garden.

Model 02 — Advanced

The Separation Toilet

How it works

A liquid and solid separator collects urine separately, which is used directly as a nitrogen-rich fertiliser. Solids compost separately into rich humus.

✓ Produces two distinct soil inputs. Zero water. No chemical fertiliser needed — ever.

The full loop

The circle is complete

What was once a problem becomes a resource. Human waste → compost → fertile soil → food → community health. No water wasted. No chemicals added. No humus lost.

Why it matters

A sustainable decision on every level

💧

Preserves drinking water

On the hill of Ambohibe, water is rare. Dry toilets save 12m³ per person per year — water that stays clean for drinking, cooking, and hygiene.

🌱

Feeds the soil

Composted waste returns humus and nutrients to the land — eliminating the need for chemical fertilisers and linking directly to the PERMADA garden.

🔬

Protects public health

Open defecation and unmanaged wastewater cause disease. Education on sanitation and hygiene breaks the cycle — protecting children and families.
