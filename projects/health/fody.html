FODY

Growing food. Delivering food. Until no family in southern Madagascar goes hungry.

UN Sustainable Development Goal

End hunger. Achieve food security. Promote sustainable agriculture.

Madagascar is one of the world's most acute hunger frontlines. Climate change has made the south nearly uninhabitable for subsistence farming. FODY is Education for Madagascar's direct commitment to SDG 2 — not as a statement, but as two ongoing actions on the ground.

Years of drought. Failing harvests. Desertified land. In the south of Madagascar, this is not a crisis — it is the condition.

KERE — the Malagasy word for famine. Not an event, but a cycle. Families who depended on the land for generations can no longer feed their children. FODY exists to break that cycle.

The heart of FODY

Permaculture food production

Emergency distribution keeps families alive today. But the centre of FODY is something more lasting — gardens that restore the land itself, so families can grow their own food, season after season.

The method

Land that was dust,
growing food again

FODY grows food through the permaculture technique pioneered by PERMADA — mounds of branches, leaves and dry vegetation that decompose into rich humus. No chemical fertilisers. No irrigation. Just the land, restored.

Within a single season, desertified ground becomes a productive garden — and families are trained to maintain and expand it themselves.

Beans, lentils, peas and vegetables — chemical-free
Humus-based soil that renews itself each year
Families trained to grow independently
The harvest

Food that feeds —
and food that sustains

Pulses are at the heart of the FODY garden. Nutrient-dense, long-storing, and crucially nitrogen-fixing — they feed the next crop as they feed the family. Food security designed to compound: every harvest leaves the land richer than it found it.

Where FODY works

The far south — Madagascar's most climate-exposed region

The Androy and Anosy regions receive some of the lowest rainfall in the country. Drought cycles have intensified with climate change, destroying harvests for years on end — turning land that once fed families into dust.

FODY's distribution reaches across the Ambovombe Androy District and Commune, in partnership with a local Malagasy association who coordinate delivery on the ground.

"Climate change and the lack of rain for several years are the root causes of the desertification of the land and the famine affecting thousands in southern Madagascar."

The long-term answer

Hunger ends when the land can feed its people again.

PERMADA restores desertified soil into living, fertile ground — without chemicals, without dependency on external inputs. As it scales, families across Madagascar gain the ability to grow enough food to feed themselves permanently. FODY bridges the gap while that work takes root.
