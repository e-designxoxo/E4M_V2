Our mission

Educate one woman, and you change a generation

When a woman learns to read, to earn, and to lead, the change doesn't stop with her. It reaches her children, her household, and her whole community.

In rural Madagascar, women are too often left out of education and economic life. The School for Women exists to change that — equipping women with literacy, practical skills, and the confidence to build a future on their own terms.

Since 2019 328 women across 4 villages have joined the programme — and the circle keeps widening.

Life at the school

Learning, together

A classroom that feels like home

For many women, the School is the first place they have been able to learn freely — without judgement, and at their own pace. What begins as a lesson in reading becomes a community of women who lift each other forward.

What women learn

A programme built for independence

The School for Women teaches far more than reading. Each woman builds a foundation of skills — practical, financial, and personal — that lets her stand on her own and lift those around her.

A learner's journey

From the first class to leading the way

Every woman follows the same path — from her first day in the classroom to the day she passes what she has learned on to others.

01

She enrols

A woman joins the programme — often the first time she has set foot in a classroom. No prior schooling is required, and no one is turned away.

02

She learns

Through regular classes, she builds literacy, numeracy, and practical skills — at a pace that fits her life and her family.

03

She practises

Skills are put to work: a first letter written, a budget kept, a craft sold. Confidence grows with every small success.

04

She leads

She steps into new roles — earning an income, supporting her children's schooling, and becoming a voice in her community.

The people behind the school

Led by women, for women

The School for Women is guided by mentors who know the community from the inside — women who have walked the same path, and who teach not just skills, but belief.

Classes are run in partnership with local educators, so the programme is rooted in the language, culture, and daily reality of the women it serves.

"Every woman who learns becomes a teacher to those around her. That is how a single classroom changes a village."
— The School for Women
