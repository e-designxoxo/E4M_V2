Why it matters

Healthcare, within reach

In much of rural Madagascar, a doctor is hours away — and a single consultation can cost more than a family earns in a week. For many, healthcare simply isn't an option.

TOMADY exists to change that: treating illness early, before it becomes catastrophe, and keeping families well enough to learn, work, and build a future.

tomady (Malagasy) — well-being: to be strong, sound, and in good health.
At the centre

Care in practice

A place families can reach

For many in rural Madagascar, the nearest clinic is hours away. The TOMADY Health Centre brings consultation, screening, and maternal care into the heart of the community — so that catching illness early is no longer a privilege, but the norm.

Every visit is a chance to treat what's wrong today, and to prevent what could go wrong tomorrow.

The heart of TOMADY

Why health comes first

A child cannot learn on an empty, aching body.

Education for Madagascar exists to give children a future through education — but a sick child cannot attend school, and an exhausted, unwell parent cannot support one. TOMADY removes that barrier. By keeping families well, the health centre makes every other project — education, nutrition, opportunity — possible.
