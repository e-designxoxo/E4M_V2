13 projects.
The same north star.

Every project here exists to remove one barrier between a child and a quality education — directly, or through the health, food, water and climate conditions that decide whether learning is even possible.

Browse the 13 projects
How it connects

Eight goals. Thirteen projects. One ladder.

Every project answers to one primary UN Sustainable Development Goal. Together they form a coherent strategy with SDG 4 — Quality Education as the keystone.

The 2030 deadline is approaching. Field action cannot wait.

In Madagascar, poverty is rarely isolated from school attendance, child labour, illiteracy, poor health, hunger, lack of water, or environmental pressure. Our response is designed as a connected system: education is the priority, and every supporting project removes one more obstacle to learning and dignity.

Three action bases

A full fan of programmes, built around real barriers.

The projects are grouped by the three areas that most directly shape a child's future: learning, wellbeing, and the living environment.

Sustainable Development Goals

Seven field priorities, strengthened by partnerships.

The organisation prioritises Quality Education while acting on the linked conditions that decide whether a child can learn: food, health, gender equality, water, sanitation and climate resilience. Partnerships help turn this work into lasting capacity.

04

Quality Education

The central commitment: quality education for children and young adults, with trained educators and practical projects for those least likely to access learning.

02

Zero Hunger

Food security work responds to famine and drought while developing longer-term permaculture solutions.

05

Gender Equality

The School for Women supports vulnerable women and girls through health, rights, empowerment and family wellbeing.

03

Good Health and Well-being

Tomady focuses on prevention, medical screening and early care so common illnesses do not become severe barriers to learning and family stability.

13

Climate Action

Environmental education, mangrove planting and soil restoration help communities adapt to climate pressure and slow local environmental degradation.

06

Clean Water and Sanitation

Dry toilets, composting, rainwater management and sanitation education protect water and improve daily living conditions.

01

No Poverty

Education remains the starting point for reducing extreme poverty, because it affects health, employment, environment and community resilience.

17

Partnerships for the Goals

Volunteers, professional partners, donors and institutions extend the organisation's capacity where field needs and expertise meet.
