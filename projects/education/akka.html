About the project

The critical early years, taken seriously.

In Madagascar, fewer than one in three children reaches the end of primary school. The inequality starts before age six — when the human brain develops faster than at any other point in a lifetime.

AKKA Montessori School was built on a conviction: every child, regardless of their family's income, deserves an environment designed for their development. In small classes, children learn independently through hands-on materials, in a calm and respectful space grounded in Malagasy culture.

Adhering to the standards of the AMI Association Montessori Internationale, AKKA provides bilingual education combining Maria Montessori's proven child-centred pedagogy with the richness of Malagasy tradition. Children arrive curious. They leave with confidence, language, independence — and a love for learning that stays with them for life.

In 2023, Education For Madagascar created AKKA to make a greater impact — starting to care for children from an early age, all from underprivileged families. It is the first bilingual Montessori programme of its kind in the region.

Dr Maria Montessori
Founder · Montessori Method
She dedicated her life to the human being
"As a proud Montessorian parent, la casa dei Bambini was the most evident inspiration for the launch of Akanin'ny Kilonga in Antananarivo, Madagascar."
1870 Born in Chiaravalle, Italy. One of the first women to graduate from the Faculty of Medicine and Surgery in Rome — at a time when women faced immense institutional barriers.
1907 Opens la casa dei Bambini in San Lorenzo, Rome — the first Children's House. Develops hands-on educational materials that children can experience directly.
1931 Meets Mahatma Gandhi. Travels the world training educators. Her method spreads to thousands of schools across every continent.
1949 Publishes The Absorbent Mind — her spiritual and philosophical approach to the child. Nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize three times. Dies in 1952 in Noordwijk, Netherlands.

Montessori pedagogy has inspired thousands of schools worldwide. AKKA is proud to carry this tradition into Madagascar.

AMI Certified International Educator Founding Mentor, AKKA

Patricia Peterson Fontenay is the founding mentor of AKKA Montessori School. An AMI-certified educator with decades of international Montessori experience, she has guided the creation of AKKA from its earliest stages — advising on the prepared environment, educator training, and the school's pedagogical framework.

Her belief that every child, regardless of background or geography, deserves access to quality Montessori education made her partnership with Education for Madagascar a natural one. Under her guidance, AKKA meets the standards of the Association Montessori Internationale.

Our approach

How AKKA works

A prepared environment

Each classroom is intentionally designed — low shelves, natural materials, child-sized furniture. The space itself teaches order, care and independence before a single lesson begins. Children choose their work freely.

Learning through doing

There are no rote lessons, no sitting still. A child learning to count handles physical objects — beads, blocks, spindles. Abstract concepts become concrete, tangible and memorable through the hands and the body.

Rooted in Malagasy culture

Language, stories, music and rhythm are drawn from Malagasy tradition. Children become bilingual in Malagasy and French — grounding their world-view in where they come from. Trilingualism (with English) is a goal.

Strategic plan

Goals & strategic actions

The execution of the strategic plan is carried out through three core pillars.

To ensure inclusive holistic education and promote lifelong learning — Montessori as an Aid to Life.

Goal 1 — Quality Montessori programmes
AMI-certified educators & standards
One AMI-certified educator per age group. Class size of 25–30 children aged 3–6. Minimum 4 consecutive school days per week. Full Montessori materials as aids to the development and exploration of the child.
Goal 2 — Prepared Environment
Inside and outside
Child-sized furniture throughout. Outdoor practical activities — gardening, care of plants and animals. Water saving through dry toilets and rain tanks. Solar energy. Biodiversity through tree and mangrove planting.
Goal 3 — Children's languages
Trilingualism: Malagasy, French, English
An environment supporting movement, language, independence and self-discipline. Trilingualism through native-speaker guides and international partnerships. Cultural and linguistic exchange projects.

For a school worthy of trust — upholding child rights, transparency, and good governance.

Goal 4 — Child & Human Rights
Zero tolerance for abuse
Montessori Observation methodology applied at all times. Clear and strong plea against child labour and abuse of any kind. Equal registration opportunities for girls and boys.
Goal 5 — Good Governance
Accountable to families and donors
Full compliance with local health and safety law. Written procedures for continuous quality management. Financial statements audited and certified by an external auditor. Support for parents and the community.
Goal 6 — Transparency
Open and straightforward
All operations published openly. Annual reports available in French since 2017. English summaries available on request. The elected Board commits to full compliance and continuous quality improvement.

Accessible to all, thanks to the generosity at each level — free for families in need.

Goal 7 — Awareness
Children teaching children
Older children spontaneously teach younger ones. SCOLA scholarship students are hired as volunteers. Regular personal development and team-building events organised for the whole community.
Goal 8 — Public Interest
Free for families in need
Free education for children in need, with priority given to low-income but motivated families. No child should be excluded from quality early education because of their family's financial situation.
Goal 9 — National Inclusion
Advocacy for systemic change
Advocating for a massive change in national education policy through partnership with the Ministry of Education of Madagascar.
Goal 10 — International Outlook
Global partnerships
Partnerships with UN ECOSOCC, Réseau UNESCO, AMF Association Montessori de France, and AMI Association Montessori Internationale. E4M is registered with the United Nations as a Civil Society Organisation (Profile Code 686912).
