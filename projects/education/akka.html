About the project

The critical early years,
taken seriously.

In Madagascar, fewer than one in three children reaches the end of primary school. The inequality starts at the very beginning — before age six, when the human brain develops faster than at any other point in a lifetime.

AKKA Montessori School — Akanin'ny Kilonga, "the House of Children" — was built on a simple conviction: every child, regardless of their family's income, deserves an environment designed for their development. In small classes, children learn independently through hands-on materials, in a calm and respectful space grounded in Malagasy culture.

Adhering to the standards of the AMI Association Montessori Internationale, AKKA provides a bilingual education combining Maria Montessori's proven child-centred pedagogy with the richness of Malagasy tradition. Children arrive curious. They leave with confidence, language, independence — and a love for learning that stays with them for life.

Our approach

How AKKA works

01

The prepared environment

Each classroom is intentionally designed — low shelves, natural materials, child-sized furniture. The space itself teaches order, care, and independence before a single lesson begins. Children choose their work freely, building intrinsic motivation from day one.

02

Learning through doing

There are no rote lessons, no sitting still. A child learning to count handles physical objects — beads, blocks, spindles. A child learning letters traces their shapes in sand. Abstract concepts become concrete, tangible, and memorable through the hands.

03

Rooted in Malagasy culture

Language, stories, music, and rhythm are drawn from Malagasy tradition. Children become bilingual in Malagasy and French — not by abandoning who they are, but by grounding their world-view in the richness of where they come from.

A child's story

What changes when a child is truly seen

At AKKA, every child is met exactly where they are. Not ranked, not hurried, not compared. The Montessori method trusts that curiosity is the natural state of a child — the classroom's job is simply not to extinguish it.

For many of our families in Antananarivo, AKKA is the first time their child has access to a stable, nurturing learning environment. Parents notice changes quickly: their child starts asking questions, choosing activities, organising their things. These are not small things. They are the foundations of a life.

Beyond academics, children at AKKA learn practical life skills — caring for their environment, preparing snacks, resolving conflict through words. Skills that serve them far beyond the classroom walls.
