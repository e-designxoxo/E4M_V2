Career Day Awareness

CAD

Discovering the professions of the future — and the reason to get up in the morning.

Education Ages 12–22 Annual event In partnership with SESAME 50% girls

From permaculture to robotics and programming, new and essential professions of our time, the list of our guests for the third edition of "Career Day Awareness", has been drawn up based on the wishes of our children and young people, also according to the emergency that is hitting dangerously our country now.

Each year, we organise an annual project of discovery and information on careers. In 2022, our beneficiaries wanted to meet in person and discuss with a chef, a tourist Guide, a journalist and a policeman.


We do believe that Malagasy generations need to be involved in sustainability not only if we want to contribute to a better Madagascar through responsible and committed youth, but also because it is no longer an option to draw Malagasy development ignoring the preservation of Madagascar's unique biodiversity and endemic ecosystems.


Therefore, it seems to us that meeting with a hydrologist, an expert/activist on climate change and a social entrepreneur oriented to Sustainability will help them find the professions that will contribute first and foremost to the economy of a country like Madagascar.

Choosing the right profession is an investigation

The right path for a young adult sits where four questions meet: the activity they love, the one they are good at, the one the world needs, and the one they can make a living from. The combination of these four elements is their ikigai — the profession most likely to bring them lasting fulfilment. CAD exists to guide young Malagasies towards that intersection.

Why Career Day?

The professions that will build Madagascar

Each year, CAD organises a discovery event introducing children and young people to the professions of the future — from permaculture to robotics and programming — chosen from our young people's own wishes and the urgent needs of the country.

In 2022, our beneficiaries wanted to meet a chef, a tourist guide, a journalist and a policeman. We also believe Malagasy youth must be involved in sustainability — so meetings with a hydrologist, a climate activist and a sustainability-focused social entrepreneur help them find professions that serve the country's economy and its endemic ecosystems.

Vision & mission

Where we go, and how

Vision

What future are we building?

Choosing the right profession is the result of an investigation between what a young adult loves, what the world needs, what they are good at, and what they could make a living at. That combination is their ikigai — the occupation closest to lasting happiness.

Mission

What is our approach? What path do we follow?

To alleviate poverty in Madagascar by accompanying young people in the right direction — through Education, Health Education and Environmental Education — combining what they love, what they are good at, and what will sustain them later.

In partnership with

SESAME

An educational programme of excellence for underprivileged students. SESAME supports high-school graduates from difficult socio-economic backgrounds who are eager to pursue higher education, succeed in their professional integration, and commit to their country's development.

50% of those supported are girls
