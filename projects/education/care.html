The mission

We don't teach children
to use technology.

mission.swift −1 +1
 // Madagascar's youth, redefined:
−let youth = "consumers of technology"
+let youth = "creators of technology"

A consumer depends on technology. A creator shapes it. Under the mentorship of Michel Sutter, CARE students learn with professional Apple developer tools — and turn that single changed word into a changed life.

/01

Build, don't browse.

Students write real code from week one — variables, functions, structs. Not tutorials about technology, but technology itself, made by their own hands. The shift from passive screen-time to active creation changes how a child sees every device they'll ever touch.

/02

Professional tools, real stakes.

Swift Playground, Swift and Xcode — the same ecosystem used by Apple's own engineers. Children sense immediately when they're given toys instead of tools. CARE gives them the real thing, and they rise to it.

/03

Solutions for Madagascar, by Madagascar.

The goal is not to export talent to Silicon Valley. It is to equip Malagasy youth with the computational thinking and creative confidence to build solutions for their own country's challenges — in their own language, on their own terms.

Michel leads CARE with professional software development expertise, adapting industry-grade tools and methods to the specific context of Malagasy children aged 12 and above.

Swift Playground

Apple's visual, gamified coding environment. Students engage with interactive challenges, moving from pattern-matching to writing real Swift code — with zero prior experience needed. The intuitive interface removes the barrier of entry while teaching genuine programming concepts: loops, conditionals, functions, objects.

Beginner group — 12 students
Swift + Xcode

The same tools used at Apple, Spotify and Airbnb. Advanced students design interfaces, write production-quality code, and compile working applications. Projects are personal — students build apps that solve problems they see in their own community.

Advanced group — 5 students
Why this stack?

Apple's ecosystem was chosen deliberately: it is cohesive, progressively structured from Playground to Xcode, and produces genuinely employable skills. The same progression is used in Apple's own education programmes worldwide — from primary school to university.
